1. Most items identified by a parrot in three minutes

The pet: Apollo, an African grey parrot.

The record: In 2024, the intelligent and vocal Apollo set a viral record by identifying 12 objects in three minutes. Videos of the parrot's clever and entertaining communication skills earned him millions of followers across social media, solidifying his celebrity status.

2. Longest tongue on a living dog

The pet: Zoey, a Labrador and German Shepherd mix.

The record: Zoey's tongue measures 12.7 cm (5 inches), longer than a can of soda. Her owners, who got her as a 6-week-old puppy, noticed her tongue constantly stuck out. The viral video of her panting with her massive tongue has been viewed millions of times, with her owners admitting she leaves "a big slobber mark on black pants".

3. Longest tail on a living domestic cat

The pet: Mr. Pugsley Addams, a Maine Coon.

The record: In early 2025, Mr. Pugsley Addams was confirmed to have the longest tail on a living domestic cat, measuring 46.99 cm (18.5 inches). Videos of the fluffy feline and his impressively long tail went viral on social media platforms like YouTube and Facebook, delighting cat lovers.

4. Most tennis balls in mouth by a dog

The pet: Finley, a Golden Retriever from New York.

The record: Finley went viral in 2020 after setting the record for holding six tennis balls in his mouth at once, beating the previous record of five. His owner chronicled the adorable, viral journey of getting the record officially verified, which involved photos, videos, and witness statements.

5. Shortest dog living

The pet: Pearl, a Chihuahua.

The record: Pearl, measuring just 9.14 cm (3.59 inches) tall, captured hearts worldwide when she was announced as the world's shortest dog. Her diminutive size, comparable to a popsicle stick, made her an instant sensation on social media and news broadcasts.

6. Most tricks by a cat in one minute

The pet: Didga, a rescue cat from Australia.

The record: Didga became a viral star for performing an impressive 20 tricks in just 60 seconds. The videos of her skateboarding, giving high-fives, and performing other complex tricks were shared widely, showcasing that cats can be trained just like dogs.

7. Most basketball slam dunks by a rabbit in one minute

The pet: Bini, a Holland Lop.

The record: Bini the Bunny wowed the internet by performing seven basketball slam dunks in one minute, earning him a Guinness World Record. Videos of the talented rabbit dribbling and shooting the tiny ball were widely shared on YouTube, attracting millions of views.

8. Tallest dog living

The pet: Kevin, a Great Dane.

The record: Named after the character in Home Alone, Kevin, standing at 0.97 meters (3 ft 2 in) tall, became a viral hit when videos of him trying to squeeze into small spaces were shared. His size, combined with his gentle and goofy nature, has made him a fan favorite.

9. Fastest tortoise

The pet: Bertie, a leopard tortoise.

The record: Bertie became a viral sensation after smashing the record for the fastest tortoise. The BBC and other news outlets shared the story of the "Usain Bolt of tortoises," who sped through an 18-foot course in just 19.59 seconds, a speed that amazed millions.

10. World's richest pet

The pet: Gunther VI, a German Shepherd.

The record: While technically a series of trusts and not a traditional record, Gunther VI went viral for his inheritance of a fortune reportedly worth over $400 million. The story of the lavish dog, his Miami mansion, and a Netflix documentary fueled global curiosity about his privileged life.
